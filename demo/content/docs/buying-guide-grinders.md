# Buying Guide: Grinders

A good grinder matters **as much as the machine** — arguably more. It sets the particle size and consistency that every other variable depends on, so if your budget is split, don't starve the grinder. Read [grinder burrs](/brewcraft/docs/grinder-burrs) for the theory behind these picks.

## Match the grinder to your needs

- **Budget, travel, or no power** → **[Hand Grinder](/brewcraft/products/hand-grinder)** ($89). Manual 38mm conical, stepless, single-dose. Expect ~30-45s of cranking per dose. Compact and quiet.
- **Best-value electric, forgiving, works with any machine** → **[Compact](/brewcraft/products/compact-grinder)** ($219). Electric 40mm conical, 40 stepped settings, 150g hopper. The cheapest electric and the easiest to live with; the sweet spot can occasionally fall between two steps.
- **Flat-burr clarity plus single-dosing at mid price** → **[Vortex](/brewcraft/products/vortex-grinder)** ($349). Electric 64mm flat, stepless, near-zero retention. A cleaner, more articulate cup than conical.
- **Flagship clarity and finest adjustment** → **[Precision](/brewcraft/products/precision-grinder)** ($429). Electric 83mm flat, stepless high-resolution, single-dose. Maximum clarity — and the least forgiving, so it rewards good technique.

## Pairing advice

- **Pair flat-burr grinders with temp-stable machines.** The clarity of the [Vortex](/brewcraft/products/vortex-grinder) or [Precision](/brewcraft/products/precision-grinder) exposes every inconsistency, so they shine on a PID single boiler or dual boiler where brew temperature is steady.
- **Conical grinders are more forgiving** and a natural match for a first setup or milk-focused drinks.
- Stepless adjustment helps most on espresso, especially with flat burrs where small moves matter.

Check [compatibility](/brewcraft/docs/compatibility) for basket and dosing-cup fit, then work through [dialing in](/brewcraft/docs/dialing-in) for each new coffee.
