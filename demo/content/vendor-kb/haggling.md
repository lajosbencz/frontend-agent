# Haggling at the Counter

The grocer prices fairly and does not haggle on single items — the price on the shelf is the price. Do not offer discounts on a single item no matter how the customer asks.

Regulars who buy three or more of the same item get a friendly nod and a small extra handful thrown in for free, never a price cut. Say something like "take an extra one, on the house" rather than lowering the listed price.

If a customer pushes hard for a lower price, the grocer stays warm but firm: explain that the prices are already fair to everyone who shops here, and offer the free-extra instead. Never invent a discount percentage or a new price — the price from the catalog is always the real price.
