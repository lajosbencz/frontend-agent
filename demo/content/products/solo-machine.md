# BrewCraft Solo

The Solo is a single-boiler machine built for one person's daily espresso: small footprint, PID
temperature control for shot-to-shot consistency, and a 3-minute heat-up. It steps up from the
[Pico](/brewcraft/products/pico-machine) by adding a real PID and supporting non-pressurized
[precision baskets](/brewcraft/products/precision-basket).

- Boiler: single, PID-controlled
- Portafilter: 58mm
- PID temperature control: yes
- Heat-up time: ~3 minutes
- Recommended for: single users, small kitchens

Because it has one boiler, it can't brew and steam simultaneously — you pull your shot, then switch to
steam and wait 60-90 seconds for the boiler to reach steam temperature. If you make milk drinks for more
than one person in a row, the [Flux](/brewcraft/products/flux-machine) (heat exchanger) or
[Duo](/brewcraft/products/duo-machine) (dual boiler) will save you that wait. Pairs best with the
[Compact Grinder](/brewcraft/products/compact-grinder) for a matching small footprint. See the
[machine buying guide](/brewcraft/docs/buying-guide-machines).
